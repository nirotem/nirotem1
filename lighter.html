The Lighter Side

Expeditions & Wanderlust

I have a passion for long-distance exploration. Notable journeys include a six-week solo trek through the Altai Mountains in Western Mongolia and three months crossing the rugged South Island of New Zealand on foot. These immersion experiences sparked my interest in the diversity of social scripts long before my career in sociology.

Peaks & Theory

While exploring the Pamir Mountains in Tajikistan, I encountered Friedrich Engels Peak. With Marx Peak located nearby, it felt like a serendipitous sign foreshadowing my eventual path into social theory and research. My travels continue to inform my perspective on global institutional norms.

Reading & Cinema

I value literature that challenges perspectives—favorites include Karel Čapek's The Gardener's Year and Murakami's Kafka on the Shore. In cinema, I prefer the curated depth of Mubi, with works like Drive My Car and Flee staying with me long after the credits roll.

Music & Spotify

Music is an essential companion for my research and writing. My tastes span from Jazz and Classical to Folk legends like Bob Dylan and contemporary Indie bands. I enjoy curating soundtracks for different research phases.
