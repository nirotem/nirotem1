The Lighter Side

Academia is a central part of my life, but it is not all that occupies my mind. I value meaningful engagement with human complexity—be it through physical exploration of the world, literature, or cinema.

Expeditions & Wanderlust

On Foot & Two Wheels

I have a lifelong passion for the great outdoors, specifically backpacking and cycle touring. Over the years, these trips have grown from monthly overnight hikes to long-distance solo expeditions.

Notable journeys include a six-week solo trek in Western Mongolia and three months crossing the South Island of New Zealand on foot. These experiences sparked my interest in different cultures long before my sociological career began, involving endless cups of kumis, tea, and vodka with nomads.

Peaks & Theory

During a trip to Tajikistan, I encountered Friedrich Engels Peak. There is a Marx Peak nearby as well, which I didn't have the chance to see on that trip—perhaps a sign that foreshadowed my current sociological career. My wanderlust is about the physical challenge but also the cultural and social engagement with human complexity.

The Library & The Screen

Reading

When I'm not in the field or the archive, I turn to fiction to spark my sociological imagination. Recent favorites include:

  • The Gardener's Year by Karel Čapek
  • Kafka on the Shore by Haruki Murakami
  • The Briefcase by Hiromi Kawakami
  • Lohikäärmekylpy by Eeva Tikka

Cinema

I find mainstream streaming services often confusing with too many bad choices, so I frequently turn to Mubi for "arty" and thoughtful cinema. Recent titles that stayed with me:

  • Drive My Car (2021)
  • Compartment No. 6 (2021)
  • Flee (2021)
  • Close (2022)
  • Storkman (2020)

Music

Music is a constant companion while I read and write. My tastes range from Jazz and Classical to Folk (Bob Dylan) and Indie. One of the best live experiences I've ever had was Arcade Fire at the Montreux Jazz Festival in 2011.
