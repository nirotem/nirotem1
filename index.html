Dr. Nir Rotem

The Hebrew University of Jerusalem

Sociologist & Researcher

I am a sociologist examining the construction and transformation of social knowledge and the contestations involved in its global circulation. My work lies at the intersection of transnational sociology, the sociology of knowledge, and political sociology.

Currently, I serve as a Research Fellow at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem. My research agenda is driven by an interest in the role of institutions and the dynamics between culture and knowledge. I am particularly interested in the plurality of global scripts and the dynamics involved when diverse sets of knowledge arrive at different social settings.

I earned my PhD from the University of Minnesota (2022). My doctoral dissertation, titled Humanitarianism, Development, and Human Rights: The Boundaries of Social Fields in Today's World Society, investigated shifts in the humanitarian idea through a historical ethnography of the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR). I utilize a diverse methodological toolkit, encompassing historical ethnography, archival work, and quantitative approaches including regression modeling and machine learning.

Research Interests

  • Sociological Neoinstitutionalism & World Society Theory
  • Global Rise of Illiberalism (Judicial Independence & Academic Freedom)
  • Sociology of Knowledge & Culture
  • Sociology of Science & Higher Education
  • Humanitarianism & Collective Aid
