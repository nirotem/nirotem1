Sociologist & Researcher

I am a sociologist examining the construction and transformation of social knowledge and the contestations involved in its global circulation. Currently, I am a Guest Professor at the University of Graz (2025) and a Research Fellow at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem.

Research Interests

My work lies at the intersection of transnational sociology, the sociology of knowledge, and political sociology. I am particularly interested in:

  • The global rise of illiberalism and its institutional drivers.
  • Sociological neoinstitutionalism and world society theory.
  • Sociology of science, higher education, and computational culture.
  • The transformation of humanitarianism and human rights.

I earned my PhD from the University of Minnesota (2022). My dissertation investigated the historical transformation of the humanitarian field through the case of the UNHCR.
