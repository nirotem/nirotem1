Dr. Nir Rotem

The Hebrew University of Jerusalem

Sociologist & Researcher

I am a sociologist examining the construction and transformation of social knowledge and the contestations involved in its global circulation. My research focuses on the transformation of and contestations around social knowledge at the global level. Specifically, I examine the plurality of global scripts and the dynamics involved when diverse sets of knowledge arrive at different social settings.

Currently, I am a David and Rosa Orzen Research Fellow and a Lady Davis postdoctoral fellow at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem. I earned my PhD from the University of Minnesota in 2022. My research agenda is driven by an interest in the role of institutions and the dynamics between culture and knowledge. I utilize a diverse methodological toolkit, encompassing historical ethnography, archival work, and quantitative approaches including survey analysis, regression modeling, and machine learning.

Looking ahead, I am eager to delve into the field of natural language processing, recognizing its potential to bridge the divide between traditional text-based coding and the growing importance of big data. I propose addressing the heterogeneity of contemporary global society by developing new measures, such as a "Think Tank count," to gain a deeper understanding of the shaping and spread of ideas and policies worldwide.
