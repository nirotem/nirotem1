Research Projects

Here is a selection of projects I am currently working on. My research explores the global circulation of cultural scripts, the institutional mechanisms of science, and the rising contestations around liberal principles—including their inner tensions and comparative measurement. Feel free to reach out for collaborations!

Ongoing Research

Illiberalism, Higher Education, and Academic Freedom

This project examines the growing trend of illiberal backlash through a neo-institutional lens. I investigate the connection between pressures on academic freedom and distrust in science globally. Using cross-national survey data, I analyze whether individuals' beliefs align with or decouple from their countries' policies, finding that science functions as a contested terrain in liberal societies and a locus of consensus in illiberal ones.

Computational Culture in Science

In collaboration with Liron Shani, I study how patterns of scientific knowledge are transformed by the computational revolution. We analyze cases in the life sciences and digital humanities to argue that computation is more than a technical toolkit; it entails a distinct mode of thinking fostering an emergent culture that cuts across the traditional science-humanities divide.

Scientific Productivity in US Universities

Working with Keith Goldstein and Gad Yair, this project unpacks the inner workings of American science using a unique dataset covering publication output across hundreds of research universities. We evaluate the "three cultures" hypothesis, demonstrating that scientific work is dominated by distinct productivity norms in the natural sciences, social sciences, and humanities.

Completed Research

The Dropout Study

For several years I served as a Co-PI (with Gad Yair) in a student attrition study at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem. Utilizing a decade of administrative data and machine learning models, we investigated the "riddle of the existential dropout"—students who leave despite adequate academic records. Our findings on affirmative action showed that students admitted through these routes graduate at similar rates to the general population, leading us to advocate for wider university access.

Illiberalism and Judicial Independence

A longitudinal study (1960-2018) tracking the global erosion of judicial independence. The findings reveal a growing divide between states aligned with liberal world society and those connected to illiberal international organizations, highlighting the institutional factors driving this global shift.

Humanitarianism & the UNHCR (Dissertation)

My doctoral research involved a historical ethnography of the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR). By tracking flows of knowledge, I pinpointed changes in the humanitarian field and recognized the infiltration of ideas from international development and human rights, interpreting these shifts through an integration of world society and field theories.

The "Other" Other: Anti-Semitic and Anti-Muslim Views

In collaboration with Joseph Gerteis, we scrutinized white anti-Semitic and anti-Muslim views in America. Using nationally representative survey data, we discovered that such exclusionary attitudes are strongly correlated with political and religious affiliations, linking ethno-religious exclusion to the broader dynamics of contemporary politics.
