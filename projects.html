Research Projects

Ongoing Projects

Illiberalism, Higher Education, and Academic Freedom

Examining the connection between pressures on academic freedom and distrust in science using cross-national survey data to understand how individuals' beliefs align with or decouple from national policies.

Trust in Science and Illiberalism

A comparative study on how science functions as a contested terrain in liberal societies versus a locus of consensus in illiberal regimes, analyzing the cultural mechanisms that sustain these institutions.

Computational Culture in Science

(With Liron Shani) Analyzing the cases of life sciences and digital humanities to study how scientific knowledge patterns are changed by technology and emerging new social interactions.

Scientific Productivity in US Universities

(With Keith Goldstein and Gad Yair) Unpacking the inner workings of American science by analyzing productivity patterns across hundreds of research universities to evaluate the "three cultures" hypothesis.

Completed Projects

Illiberalism and Judicial Independence

A longitudinal panel data analysis (1960-2018) exploring the global trajectory of judicial independence and the influence of illiberal intergovernmental organizations on state activity.

Women's Empowerment in Sub-Saharan Africa

(With Elizabeth Heger Boyle) Using survey data to examine how women enact global scripts of rights, focusing on the simultaneous operation of world society and localized institutions like religion.

Dissertation: Humanitarianism, Development, and Human Rights

A historical ethnography of the UNHCR investigating shifts in the humanitarian idea and the intrusion of development and human rights knowledge into the field.

The "Other" Other

(With Joseph Gerteis) Scrutinizing white anti-Semitic and anti-Muslim views in America, linking ethnoreligious exclusion to political and religious affiliations.

The Dropout Study

(With Gad Yair) Investigating student attrition using machine learning models to identify "existential dropouts" and evaluate the success of affirmative action programs.
