Research Projects

Here is a selection of my current and completed research projects.

Ongoing Research

Illiberalism, Higher Education, and Academic Freedom

This project implements insights from sociological neoinstitutionalism to understand the growing global trend of the illiberal backlash. I examine the connection between pressures on academic freedom and distrust in science globally.

Trust in Science and Illiberalism

Building on my work on global scripts, this project examines the association between countries' democratic and illiberal orientations and citizens' trust in science.

Computational Culture in Science

In collaboration with Liron Shani, I study how patterns of scientific knowledge are changed by technology and emerging social interactions.

Scientific Productivity in US Universities

Working with Keith Goldstein and Gad Yair, we unpack the inner workings of American science using a unique dataset covering publication output across hundreds of universities.

Completed Research

The Dropout Study

Served as a CO-PI in a student attrition study at the Hebrew University. We utilized machine learning models to investigate the phenomenon of student dropout.

Illiberalism and Judicial Independence

A longitudinal study tracking the global erosion of judicial independence and the shift towards illiberalism.

Humanitarianism, Development, and Human Rights (UNHCR)

Historical ethnography of the UNHCR tracking flows of knowledge and pinpointing changes in the humanitarian field.

The "Other" Other: Anti-Semitic and Anti-Muslim Views

Analyzed survey data to study the relationship between anti-Semitic and anti-Muslim views in America.

Women's Empowerment in Sub-Saharan Africa

Examined how women enact global scripts of rights focusing on household decision-making across 25 countries.
