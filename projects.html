Research Projects

My work primarily examines global knowledge dynamics, the rise of illiberalism and current contestations, and the study of higher education from a sociology of science perspective. Here is a selection of projects I am currently (or have finished) working on. I am happy to collaborate (or consider collaborations) around these issues.

Ongoing Research

Illiberalism, Higher Education, and Academic Freedom

I examine the connection between pressures on academic freedom and distrust in science globally. Using cross-national survey data, I analyze whether individuals' beliefs align with or decouple from the policies adopted by their countries.

Trust in Science and Illiberalism

A comparative study investigating how science functions as a contested terrain in liberal societies versus a locus of consensus in illiberal regimes.

Computational Culture in Science

In collaboration with Liron Shani, I study how patterns of scientific knowledge are changed by technology and emerging social interactions.

Scientific Productivity in US Universities

Working with Keith Goldstein and Gad Yair, we unpack the inner workings of American science using a unique dataset covering publication output across hundreds of universities.

Completed Research

The Dropout Study

Served as CO-PI (with Gad Yair) in a student attrition study at the Hebrew University. Utilized machine learning models to investigate the phenomenon of student dropout at both undergraduate and master’s levels.

Illiberalism and Judicial Independence

A longitudinal study (1960-2018) tracking the global erosion of judicial independence and the shift towards illiberalism.

Humanitarianism, Development, and Human Rights (UNHCR)

Historical ethnography of the UNHCR tracking flows of knowledge and pinpointing changes in the humanitarian field.

The "Other" Other: Anti-Semitic and Anti-Muslim Views

Analyzed survey data to study the relationship between anti-Semitic and anti-Muslim views in America.

Women's Empowerment in Sub-Saharan Africa

Examined how women enact global scripts of rights focusing on household decision-making across 25 countries.
